Touti Game Rules

Touti is a classic trick-taking card game played with a Spanish deck. Master the bidding, trump selection, and strategic play to outscore your opponents.

The Deck

Touti is played with a 40-card Spanish deck consisting of four suits:

Oros (Coins)
Copas (Cups)
Espadas (Swords)
Bastos (Clubs)

Each suit contains 10 cards: 1 (Ace), 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 10 (Sota), 11 (Caballo), and 12 (Rey).

Teams and Players

Touti is a four-player partnership game where players form two teams:

  • Team 1: Players 1 and 3 (sitting opposite each other)
  • Team 2: Players 2 and 4 (sitting opposite each other)

Partners work together to win tricks and achieve the highest score.

Game Setup

At the start of each round:

  1. The deck is shuffled
  2. Each player is dealt 10 cards
  3. The bidding phase begins

Bidding and Engagement

The bidding phase (called "engagement") determines who will choose the trump suit and sets the minimum points that team must score.

How Bidding Works:

  • Bidding starts from the player to the right of the dealer and proceeds counterclockwise
  • Valid bids are ascending values: 80, 90, 100, 110, 120, etc.
  • Each player can either bid higher than the current bid or pass
  • Once you pass, you cannot bid again in that round
  • The last player to make a valid bid wins the engagement

Choosing Trump (Fire):

The player who wins the bidding gets to choose the trump suit (called "fire" or "touti"). The trump suit beats all other suits during trick play.

Trick Play

After the trump suit is chosen, the trick-taking phase begins. The player who won the bidding leads the first trick.

Playing Cards:

  1. Follow suit: If you have a card of the led suit, you must play it
  2. Play trump: If you don't have the led suit but have trump cards, you must play a trump
  3. Play any card: Only if you have neither the led suit nor trump can you play any other card

Winning Tricks:

  • The highest trump card wins the trick
  • If no trump is played, the highest card of the led suit wins
  • The winner of each trick leads the next trick

Scoring

Points are awarded based on the cards won in tricks and special declarations.

Card Values:

  • Ace (1): 11 points
  • Three (3): 10 points
  • Rey (12): 4 points
  • Caballo (11): 3 points
  • Sota (10): 2 points
  • Other cards (2, 4, 5, 6, 7): 0 points

The total card points in the deck equal 120 points.

Last Trick Bonus:

The team that wins the last trick of the round receives an additional 10 points.

Singing (Declarations):

Players can declare certain card combinations (called "singing") for additional points, subject to game-specific limits. Common declarations include:

  • Four of a kind: Having all four cards of the same rank
  • Trump sequence: Consecutive trump cards

Note: Specific singing rules and point values may vary by local traditions.

Winning the Round

After all tricks are played, points are tallied:

  • If the bidding team scores at least their bid amount, they earn their actual score
  • If they fail to meet their bid, they lose points equal to their bid
  • The opposing team always scores the points they collected

Winning the Game

The game continues for multiple rounds. The first team to reach a predetermined point total (commonly 1000 points) wins the game.

Strategy Tips

  • Count cards: Keep track of which high-value cards have been played
  • Communicate with your partner: Use legal plays to signal strength in certain suits
  • Manage trump cards: Don't waste trump on low-value tricks
  • Bid wisely: Consider your hand strength and trump potential before bidding
  • Control the last trick: The 10-point bonus can be crucial
